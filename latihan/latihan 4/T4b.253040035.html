The most unique volcano in Indonesia's ring of fire:

Written by Paul Nelson. October 30, 2025.

Mount Bromo is located in East Java and is part of the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park. Known as one of Indonesia’s most iconic volcanoes, Bromo features an active crater that constantly emits white smoke. Surrounding it is a vast sea of sand called the Tengger Sand Sea, creating a landscape that feels almost otherworldly. At sunrise, the golden rays illuminate the mist and mountain peaks, offering a breathtaking view — a moment that has become the main attraction for travelers from around the world.

Beyond its natural beauty, Mount Bromo is also rich in cultural values. On its slopes live the Tengger people, descendants of the Majapahit Kingdom who still preserve their ancient traditions. One of their most famous traditions is the Yadnya Kasada Ceremony, an annual ritual in which the community throws offerings of agricultural produce into Bromo’s crater as a tribute to Sang Hyang Widi. This tradition symbolizes gratitude and respect for nature, while also reflecting the deep connection between humans and their environment.

Bromo is a perfect blend of natural beauty, culture, and spirituality. There are few places in the world that offer such a breathtaking sunrise experience while allowing visitors to witness living local traditions. Its easy access, stunning scenery, and the warm hospitality of the Tengger people make Bromo a must-visit destination at least once in a lifetime. Mount Bromo is not only the pride of East Java but also a symbol of Indonesia's beauty and harmony within the world's Ring of Fire.
